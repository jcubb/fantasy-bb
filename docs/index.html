What This Tool Does

A draft-day companion for an AL-only rotisserie league (15 teams). It shows depth charts and CBS AL rankings side-by-side so you can see who is starting, check players off as they're drafted, build your roster, and track how your team projects in the five roto categories for batters and pitchers.

How to Use It

  • Batters / Pitchers tabs — depth-chart grid (starters bold, backups gray) across all 15 AL teams. Hover a player for rank, eligibility, projections, and injury notes.
  • Check a player to mark them drafted. An Assign modal opens so you can place them in a roster slot. Uncheck to un-draft and remove from the roster.
  • Ranked list (below the grid) — all players sorted by CBS AL rank, with position filter and name search. Same checkboxes as the grid.
  • / key — quick search overlay. Type a name, use ↑/↓ to navigate, Enter to select.
  • Roster panel (right side) — shows your 14 batter + 9 pitcher slots. Click a name to reassign. Clear roster removes slot assignments without un-drafting players.
  • Injuries tab — all players with active injury notes, sorted by rank, with a suggested replacement from the depth chart.
  • Last Week tab — recent MLB.com news headlines by team, filtered to player-relevant stories from the past 7 days.
  • My Team tab — projects your drafted players' per-player stat averages against the weighted pool of AL starters (Q1/Median/Q3 and percentile columns). Lower percentile = better.
  • Run Scraper button — triggers a GitHub Actions workflow that refreshes depth charts, rankings, and news (takes ~5 min). Reload the page afterward.
  • Reset Draft — clears all drafted marks and roster assignments. Cannot be undone.

Draft state (checked players, roster assignments) is saved in your browser's localStorage and is local to your browser only — other people opening this page see their own independent state.

Data Sources

  • Depth charts — scraped from CBS Sports fantasy baseball depth chart pages (cbssports.com/fantasy/baseball/depth-chart/), one page per position. AL teams identified by table heading. Refreshed daily at 7 AM ET via GitHub Actions, or manually via the Run Scraper button.
  • Rankings — CBS Sports AL-only rotisserie top-300, scraped at the same time as depth charts.
  • Projections — CBS Sports Rest-of-Season projected stats, manually copied from the CBS stats page and parsed locally. Not refreshed automatically; last updated when the projections were last run.
  • Position eligibility — calculated from SABR/Lahman historical fielding data. A player is eligible at a position if they played 20+ games there in the 2025 season. This is not official CBS eligibility.
  • Injury notes — pulled from the CBS depth chart pages alongside player listings. Notes are filtered by keyword to suppress non-injury blurbs, but the filter is imperfect.

Warnings & Limitations

⚠ Use at your own risk. This tool is a personal project built for convenience, not a production system. The following are known limitations:
  • Eligibility is approximate. Position eligibility is calculated from Lahman fielding data and does not match CBS's official eligibility rules exactly. Always verify eligibility on CBS before making roster decisions.
  • Projections may be stale. Rest-of-season projections are refreshed manually and could be weeks out of date. Treat them as rough guides, not current forecasts.
  • Scraper accuracy. Depth charts and rankings are parsed from CBS's HTML structure. If CBS changes their page layout, the scraper may produce incomplete or incorrect data without warning.
  • Name matching. Player names are reconciled between data sources using fuzzy normalization. Occasionally a player may be mismatched or missing projections/eligibility data, especially for players with recent name changes, Jr./Sr. suffixes, or accented characters.
  • No data validation. Projection values and depth chart positions are displayed as-is from the source. They have not been independently verified.
