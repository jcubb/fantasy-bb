Draft Dashboard Ideas — Live Auction Analytics
===============================================

Goal: real-time dashboard for a fast-paced AL-only rotisserie auction draft.
Track where supply is ample or tight, and whether it's a "good time" to bid
based on whether the market is running hot or cold.

All of the following build on data already in the app:
- draftPrice (actual auction prices paid)
- salaries (CBS AL-only projected salaries)
- draftTimes (ISO timestamp on every pick — preserves temporal order)
- roster state (draftedBy, open slot counts per team)


PRIORITY 1 — Inflation Rate (highest impact, single number)
------------------------------------------------------------
Formula: Total league money remaining / Total projected salary value remaining
  - League money remaining = sum of (260 - spent) across all teams
  - Projected value remaining = Left $ All Bat + Left $ All Pit (already in table)

Interpretation:
  - Inflation 1.25x → bid up to 1.25 × projected salary on any player
  - Inflation < 1.0 → market is underpaying, bid near projection for value

Display: headline number above the Salary Pool table, or a footer row.
This is the single most actionable number during a live auction.


PRIORITY 2 — Recent Market Premium (draft tempo signal)
--------------------------------------------------------
Uses timestamps. Compute: (draftPrice - projectedSal) / projectedSal
for recent picks (last ~10) vs. overall-draft average.

Example display:
  Market: +18% over projection  (last 10 picks: +28% ↑)

When recent premium is rising → market running hot → wait, don't bid.
When recent premium drops below overall average → value opportunities open up.


PRIORITY 3 — Team Budget Pressure (per-team $/slot)
----------------------------------------------------
For each team in the League tab: budget remaining / open roster slots.

  $120 left, 8 slots = $15/slot → dangerous bidder, will compete hard
  $25 left, 8 slots  = $3/slot  → cash-strapped, hunting bargains only

Already partially visible from the budget footer per card, but distilling
it to a single $/slot number per card makes it immediately actionable.


PRIORITY 4 — Position Scarcity Ratio (extend existing table)
-------------------------------------------------------------
Already have Open and Left# in the Salary Pool table. Add ratio: Open / Left#.

  C:  Open=8,  Left#=6  → ratio 1.33 → more demand than supply → prices stay high
  OF: Open=20, Left#=45 → ratio 0.44 → supply >> demand → can afford to wait

Could be a new row in the table, or replace/augment one of the existing rows.


PRIORITY 5 — Stranded Salary Warning
--------------------------------------
Teams that are nearly full but still have significant budget left can't spend
that money — yet it inflates prices for everyone else bidding on the remaining
players. Identifying teams with high $ remaining but few/no open slots gives
a cleaner picture of actual available competition at each position.

Could flag these teams in the League tab (e.g., a subtle indicator on the card).


Implementation notes:
- Priorities 1 and 2 are the most impactful pair: together they answer
  "what should I bid?" and "is now a good time to bid?"
- All five are fast to compute from existing in-memory state.
- Timestamps are stored as ISO strings; temporal order is available for
  computing rolling averages over the last N picks.
